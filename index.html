Chicken & Summer Vegetable Tostadas

A south-of-the-border supper is ready in a snap thanks to quick-cooking chicken tenders. The tostadas can easily become soft tacos if you skip broiling the tortillas. Serve with black beans

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons canola oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon Kosher salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 12 ounces chicken breast tenders
  • 1 cup chopped red onion
  • 1 cup fresh corn kernels
  • 1 cup chopped zucchini
  • 1/2 cup >salsa verde
  • 3 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • 4 (8-inch) fat-free flour tortillas
  • Cooking spray
  • 3 ounces Monterey Jack cheese, shredded

How To Make It

  1. Preheat broiler

  2. Combine first 3 ingredients, stirring well. Heat oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Sprinkle the spice mixture evenly over the chicken. Add chicken to pan; saute for 3 minutes. Add onion, corn, and zucchini to pan; saute for 2 minutes. Stir in salsa and 2 tablespoons cilantro. Cook 2 minutes or until liquid almost evaporates, stirring frequently.

  3. Working with 2 tortillas at a time, arrange tortillas in a single layer on a baking sheet; lightly coat tortillas with cooking spray. Broil 3 minutes or until lightly browned. Spoon about 3/4 cup chicken mixture in the center of each tortilla; sprinkle each serving with 1/4 cup cheese. Broil an additional 2 minutes or until cheese melts. Repeat procedure with remaining tortillas, chicken mixture, and cheese. Sprinkle each serving with about 3/4 teaspoon of the remaining cilantro. Serve immediately.

Chef's Notes

This recipe originally ran in Cooking Light August, 2006 and was updated for the November, 2012 anniversary issue.
